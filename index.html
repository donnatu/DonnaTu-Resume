Hello, I am currently an undergraduate student pursuing a degree in Business Administration at the University of California, Riverside. I hope to major in Finance and become an actuary. Prior to studying at the University of California, Riverside, I attended San Gabriel High School located in San Gabriel, California.

Experience: I have had previous experience working for small businesses prior to pursuing such a degree. I was an academic tutor for an after-school program at an elementary school. Here, I tutored kids that ranged from third grade to sixth grade. I mainly focused on reading comprehension and deciphering word problems for math. The kids are usually more reserved and they like to keep to themselves because a few are shy about asking for help. With this in mind, I would always offer my help every ten to twenty minutes to ensure they are on the right track. Additionally, if I see that a few of the students are struggling, I would also create my own math problems and change up the numbers so the kids could have more problems to practice. It is like how everyone says: practice makes perfect. Unfortunately, I lost my job as an academic tutor due to the COVID-19 pandemic that resulted in my workplace to shut down. For this reason, I scouted out for internships in the summer. I found a job as a financial intern at an import and export company called MTL Express. I only worked there for four months but it was an impactful time. It was at MTL Express where I learned to prepare reports and analyze statistical data in order to make decisions to help the company. There, I also learned to master Microsoft Excel and practice reading reports. After the internship ended, I decided to stop looking for work because the government started giving out more money to the unemployed. Now, I am currently receiving money from EDD.
